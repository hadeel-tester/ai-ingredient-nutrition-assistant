# Coconut Oil

## Summary
Coconut oil is an edible oil extracted from the meat of mature coconuts, valued for cooking, baking, and skincare due to its high smoke point and solid-at-room-temperature texture. It contains about 90% saturated fat, predominantly medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs) like lauric acid, which some claim offer unique metabolic advantages over other saturated fats. [ahajournals](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.119.043052)

## Health Risks
- Systematic reviews show coconut oil raises LDL cholesterol by about 10 mg/dL compared to nontropical vegetable oils, potentially increasing cardiovascular risk. [ahajournals](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.119.043052)
- Despite HDL increases, the net effect on lipid profile does not support superiority over unsaturated oils for heart health. [bmjopen.bmj](https://bmjopen.bmj.com/content/8/3/e020167)
- High calorie density contributes to weight gain if consumed excessively, like all oils. [eufic](https://www.eufic.org/en/misinformation/article/is-coconut-oil-a-healthy-choice)
- Limited evidence for broad metabolic benefits; most saturated fat behaves like long-chain types rather than being rapidly burned. [eufic](https://www.eufic.org/en/misinformation/article/is-coconut-oil-a-healthy-choice)

## Health Benefits
- MCTs, particularly lauric acid, provide quick energy via rapid digestion and potential ketone production, useful for exercise or low-carb diets. [ui.adsabs.harvard](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2025PChRv..24..659D/abstract)
- Antimicrobial properties of lauric acid and monolaurin active against bacteria, viruses, and fungi in lab studies. [ui.adsabs.harvard](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2025PChRv..24..659D/abstract)
- High stability for high-heat cooking, producing fewer harmful compounds than polyunsaturated oils. [bmjopen.bmj](https://bmjopen.bmj.com/content/8/3/e020167)
- May support oral health through oil pulling practice, though evidence is preliminary. [healthline](https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/top-10-evidence-based-health-benefits-of-coconut-oil)

## Healthier Alternatives
- Extra virgin olive oil or rapeseed oil, rich in monounsaturated fats with stronger cardiovascular evidence. [ahajournals](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.119.043052)
- Avocado oil for high-heat cooking with better unsaturated fat profile. [bmjopen.bmj](https://bmjopen.bmj.com/content/8/3/e020167)
- Whole food fats like avocados, nuts, seeds, and olives for nutrient density and satiety.

## Allergen Info
- Not an EU top-14 allergen; coconut rarely cross-reacts with tree nut allergies despite botanical relation. [eufic](https://www.eufic.org/en/misinformation/article/is-coconut-oil-a-healthy-choice)

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: ~862 kcal
- Fat: ~100g (90% saturated, ~50% lauric acid MCT), trace polyunsaturated fats. [ahajournals](https://www.ahajournals.org/doi/10.1161/CIRCULATIONAHA.119.043052)
