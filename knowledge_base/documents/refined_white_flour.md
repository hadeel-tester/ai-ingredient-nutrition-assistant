# Refined White Flour

## Summary
Refined white flour is wheat flour with bran and germ removed through milling, leaving mostly endosperm starch and protein. This processing strips 70-80% of fiber, vitamins, and minerals found in whole wheat flour. Commonly enriched with iron, B vitamins, and folic acid to partially restore some nutrients lost during refining.

## Health Risks
- Very high glycemic index causes rapid blood sugar spikes and insulin response.
- Lacks dietary fiber leading to poor satiety and digestive health.
- Contributes to nutrient-poor calorie intake when staple in diet.
- Associated with increased risk of obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular disease in population studies.

## Health Benefits
- Long shelf life and stable baking properties.
- Smooth texture ideal for cakes, pastries, and white bread.
- Enrichment provides significant amounts of iron, folic acid, and B vitamins.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Whole wheat flour retaining bran and germ.
- Spelt, rye, or einkorn ancient grains.
- Almond flour, coconut flour, or chickpea flour for low-carb baking.

## Allergen Info
- Contains gluten; major allergen for celiac disease and wheat sensitivity.
- Top-14 EU allergen requiring clear labeling.
- Cross-contamination risk in facilities processing other grains.
