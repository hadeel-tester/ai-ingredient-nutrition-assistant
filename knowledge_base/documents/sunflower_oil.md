# Sunflower Oil

## Summary
Sunflower oil is extracted from sunflower seeds and available in high-linoleic, mid-oleic, and high-oleic varieties differing in fatty acid profiles. High-oleic versions contain 80%+ monounsaturated fats making them stable for cooking, while standard versions are high in omega-6 polyunsaturated fats. Rich source of vitamin E; commonly used for frying, baking, dressings, and processed foods.

## Health Risks
- High omega-6 content in standard varieties contributes to omega-6/omega-3 imbalance promoting inflammation when overconsumed.
- Polyunsaturated oils oxidize during high-heat cooking forming harmful aldehydes and free radicals.
- Calorie-dense; contributes to weight gain when used excessively.
- Often highly refined removing antioxidants and flavor compounds.

## Health Benefits
- High vitamin E content acts as antioxidant protecting cells from oxidative damage.
- High-oleic varieties improve cholesterol profile similar to olive oil.
- Linoleic acid supports skin health and reduces heart disease risk in moderation.
- Neutral flavor and high smoke point suitable for cooking.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Extra virgin olive oil for dressings and medium-heat cooking.
- Avocado oil for high-heat frying.
- Cold-pressed seed oils used unheated.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Rare sunflower seed allergy exists but does not typically affect oil consumers.
