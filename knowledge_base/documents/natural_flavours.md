# Natural Flavours

## Summary
Natural flavours are flavouring preparations obtained from plant, animal, microbial, or other natural sources by physical, enzymatic, or microbiological processes. EU Regulation 1334/2008 defines and authorizes their use with strict safety evaluations by EFSA for each specific flavour type. While "natural" suggests wholesome origins, some may contain allergens or processing residues requiring careful label reading.

## Health Risks
- Some natural flavours derived from allergens (nuts, soy, dairy) trigger reactions despite low concentrations.
- Essential oils in high doses can be irritants or cause photosensitivity.
- Variable composition between batches due to natural sourcing.
- Animal-derived natural flavours (castoreum, civet) raise ethical concerns.

## Health Benefits
- Often preferred by consumers seeking "clean label" products.
- Can provide complex authentic taste profiles difficult to replicate synthetically.
- Some contain beneficial phytochemicals from source materials.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Whole spices, herbs, fruits, and vegetables for flavour.
- Homemade extracts and infusions.
- "Natural identical" flavourings when vegan/allergen-free needed.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen category itself.
- Must declare if contains top-14 allergens per EU labelling rules.
- Cross-contamination risk in production facilities.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: variable (typically <100 kcal)
- Primarily volatile compounds (<1% used in foods)
- Carrier oils/solvents: 20-80%
