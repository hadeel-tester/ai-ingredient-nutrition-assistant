# Vanilla Extract

## Summary
Vanilla extract is a concentrated liquid flavoring made by macerating or percolating vanilla beans in a 35%+ alcohol (ethanol) and water solution for months, extracting over 200 aromatic compounds including vanillin, vanillic acid, and p-hydroxybenzaldehyde. Single-fold extract requires 13.35 oz beans per gallon; commercial extracts often use extract-grade beans processed via cold or warm percolation. Essential for baking, desserts, and beverages providing complex sweet, creamy, floral aroma.

## Health Risks
- Alcohol content (35%+) unsuitable for children, pregnant women, or alcohol-sensitive individuals.
- Rare contact dermatitis from bean handling during production.
- Imitation extracts (vanillin-only) lack beneficial antioxidants of pure extract.
- Propylene glycol versions may cause mild digestive discomfort in large amounts.

## Health Benefits
- Contains antioxidants (vanilloids, phenols) with anti-inflammatory properties.
- Vanilla aromatherapy reduces anxiety and improves mood.
- May support cognitive function and reduce oxidative stress.
- Natural alcohol extraction preserves heat-sensitive flavor compounds.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Vanilla bean paste or powder for alcohol-free option.
- Vanilla sugar (beans stored with sugar).
- Ground vanilla beans for texture and intense flavor.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Alcohol-derived; suitable for nut-free, gluten-free diets.
- Vanilla orchid pollen allergy extremely rare.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: ~288 kcal (from alcohol)
- Alcohol: 35-40%
- Vanillin: 0.1-0.5g
- Carbohydrates: minimal
