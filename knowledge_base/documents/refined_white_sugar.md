# Refined White Sugar

## Summary
Refined white sugar is highly purified sucrose extracted from sugar cane or sugar beet, consisting of 99.9% carbohydrates with no significant micronutrients, fiber, protein, or fat. It provides empty calories used universally in baking, beverages, and processed foods as a sweetener and preservative. WHO recommends limiting free sugars to less than 10% of total energy intake due to links with obesity, diabetes, and dental caries.

## Health Risks
- High glycemic index causes rapid blood glucose spikes and insulin surges.
- Contributes empty calories promoting weight gain and obesity.
- Strongly linked to tooth decay through bacterial fermentation producing acid.
- Excessive intake associated with increased risk of type 2 diabetes and cardiovascular disease.

## Health Benefits
- Provides quick energy source for brain and muscles.
- Stable preservative preventing microbial growth in foods.
- Reliable baking aid creating desired texture and browning through Maillard reaction.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Whole fruit providing fiber, vitamins, and slower sugar release.
- Unrefined sugars like coconut sugar or date syrup with minor minerals.
- Natural zero-calorie sweeteners like stevia or monk fruit extract.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Pure sucrose safe for all dietary restrictions.
