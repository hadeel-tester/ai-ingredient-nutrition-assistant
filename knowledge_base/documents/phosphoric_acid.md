# Phosphoric Acid (E338)

## Summary
Phosphoric acid is a mineral acid used primarily in cola beverages for sharp acidity and tangy flavor, also functioning as a preservative, pH regulator, and sequestrant in processed cheese, jams, canned goods, and baked products. EFSA established group ADI of 40 mg/kg body weight (as phosphorus) for phosphates including E338-E343. Excessive consumption from soft drinks linked to bone health concerns due to calcium-phosphate imbalance.

## Health Risks
- High intake interferes with calcium absorption potentially weakening bones and increasing osteoporosis risk.
- Frequent cola consumption associated with lower bone mineral density especially in adolescent girls.
- May contribute to kidney strain and chronic kidney disease progression in susceptible individuals.
- Tooth enamel erosion from acidic soft drinks containing phosphoric acid.

## Health Benefits
- Inhibits microbial growth extending shelf life of acidic foods.
- Provides sharp clean acidity preferred in cola-type beverages.
- Acts as dough conditioner and leavening agent in baking.
- Stabilizes processed cheese emulsions preventing separation.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Citric acid (E330) or malic acid (E296) from fruit sources.
- Natural acidity from fruit juices or vinegar.
- Carbonation alone for beverage tartness.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Mineral acid; no protein content triggering allergies.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: 0 kcal
- Phosphorus: 31.6g (pure compound)
- Provides acidity only; no macronutrients
