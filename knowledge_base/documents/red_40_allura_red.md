# Red 40 / Allura Red (E129)

## Summary
Red 40 (Allura Red AC) is a synthetic azo dye used to give a bright red color to foods, drinks, medicines, and cosmetics. It is one of the most widely used artificial colorants worldwide and is approved with an acceptable daily intake (ADI) by major regulators. Concerns focus on possible links to hyperactivity in susceptible children and rare allergic reactions, rather than acute toxicity.

## Health Risks
- May worsen hyperactivity or inattention in some children with existing behavioural disorders.
- Can trigger intolerance reactions (hives, asthma-like symptoms, headaches) in sensitive individuals.
- Animal studies suggest potential effects on gut barrier function and inflammation at high doses.
- Contains aromatic azo structures; impurities and breakdown products are tightly regulated due to theoretical carcinogenicity concerns.

## Health Benefits
- No direct health benefits; used solely for appearance and consumer appeal.
- Can help standardize product color, aiding flavour identification and branding.
- Allows reformulation away from natural colour sources that may be more allergenic (for example certain fruit or plant extracts).

## Healthier Alternatives
- Natural colours from beetroot, paprika, carrot, or anthocyanins (berry and grape extracts).
- Products with no added colours, relying on natural food appearance.
- Using smaller amounts of less intense natural color blends.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Intolerance reactions possible, especially in people with existing allergies or asthma.
- Some countries require warning labels about potential effects on activity and attention in children.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: ~0 kcal (used in very small amounts)
- Macronutrients: negligible
- Function: colour only, no vitamins or minerals
