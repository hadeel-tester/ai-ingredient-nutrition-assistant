# Carboxymethylcellulose (E466)

## Summary
Carboxymethylcellulose (CMC, E466) is a versatile food additive derived from cellulose, a plant-based fibre. It functions primarily as a thickener, stabiliser, and emulsifier in processed foods such as ice creams, sauces, gluten-free baked goods, and low-fat dairy products. While approved as safe by regulatory authorities, recent research has raised questions about potential effects on gut microbiota at high intake levels.

## Health Risks
- Animal studies suggest CMC may promote low-grade gut inflammation, bacterial dysbiosis, and increased susceptibility to colitis by altering the gut mucus layer and bacterial behaviour.
- High consumption of emulsifiers like CMC has been linked to metabolic changes including glucose intolerance and obesity in preclinical research, though human data remains limited.
- Excessive intake may contribute to digestive discomfort in sensitive individuals, though this is uncommon at typical dietary levels.
- As a highly processed additive, it provides no nutritional value and is often found in ultra-processed foods alongside other concerning ingredients.

## Health Benefits
- CMC enables the creation of low-fat and reduced-calorie products by providing creamy texture without added fat or sugar.
- In gluten-free baking, it improves dough structure and moisture retention, helping mimic the properties of gluten.
- Its stabilising properties extend shelf life and maintain product consistency in commercial foods.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Guar gum or xanthan gum, which may have milder effects on gut microbiota based on current research.
- Psyllium husk or chia seeds, which provide similar thickening benefits with added dietary fibre and prebiotic effects.
- Choosing whole, minimally processed foods reduces reliance on any commercial thickeners and emulsifiers.

## Allergen Info
- Not listed among the EU top-14 allergens.
- Derived from plant cellulose, it contains negligible protein and is unlikely to trigger allergic reactions.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: ~300 kcal (mostly indigestible fibre)
- Provides no meaningful vitamins, minerals, or macronutrients; functions purely as a texturiser
