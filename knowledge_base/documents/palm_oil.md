# Palm Oil

## Summary
Palm oil is an edible vegetable oil derived from the mesocarp of oil palm fruit, widely used in processed foods, margarine, frying, and confectionery due to its stability and semi-solid texture at room temperature. It contains about 50% saturated fat (mostly palmitic acid) making it controversial for cardiovascular health, though unrefined red palm oil retains vitamin E and carotenoids. Used in half of packaged supermarket products globally.

## Health Risks
- High saturated fat content raises LDL cholesterol more than unsaturated oils.
- Processing generates glycidyl esters and 3-MCPD, potential carcinogens identified by EFSA.
- Promotes inflammation and endothelial dysfunction in some human trials.
- Contributes significantly to total saturated fat intake in Western diets.

## Health Benefits
- Red palm oil provides vitamin E (tocotrienols) and beta-carotene (vitamin A precursor).
- Stable at high temperatures suitable for frying with lower oxidation than polyunsaturated oils.
- Contains phytosterols that may lower cholesterol absorption.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Olive oil or rapeseed oil for general cooking.
- Coconut oil if saturated fat preference (lauric acid profile).
- Avocado oil for high-heat cooking.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Rare palm pollen sensitivity in plantation workers.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: 884 kcal
- Saturated fat: 49g
- Monounsaturated: 37g
- Polyunsaturated: 9g
