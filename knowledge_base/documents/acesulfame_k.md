# Acesulfame K (E950)

## Summary
Acesulfame K is a zero-calorie artificial sweetener 200 times sweeter than sugar, heat-stable for cooking and baking, commonly blended with other sweeteners to mask its bitter aftertaste. Approved by FDA (GRAS), EFSA, and WHO/JECFA with ADI of 15 mg/kg body weight; rapidly excreted unchanged in urine. Used in diet sodas, gums, tabletop sweeteners, yogurt, and protein bars.

## Health Risks
- Bitter metallic aftertaste disliked by some consumers.
- Possible headaches or gastrointestinal discomfort in sensitive individuals.
- Animal studies showed no carcinogenicity; some observational studies suggest weak cancer associations needing further research.
- May alter gut microbiota composition at high doses in animal models.

## Health Benefits
- Zero calories and glycemic index suitable for diabetics and weight control.
- Heat stable unlike aspartame, ideal for cooking and industrial processing.
- Does not promote tooth decay; cariogenic-neutral.
- Enables palatable reduced-sugar formulations improving dietary compliance.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Steviol glycosides (E960) from stevia plant.
- Monk fruit extract for natural sweetness.
- Reducing overall sweetener use with natural fruit flavors.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Synthetic compound; no protein content triggering allergies.
