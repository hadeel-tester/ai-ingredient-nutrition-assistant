# Maltodextrin (E1400)

## Summary
Maltodextrin is a carbohydrate powder derived from starch (corn, wheat, potato), used as a thickener, filler, and carrier in processed foods, supplements, and pharmaceuticals. It has a high glycemic index, rapidly raising blood sugar, and is considered safe by regulators with no numerical ADI due to low toxicity. Some studies suggest gut microbiome effects at high doses, but typical intake poses minimal risk.

## Health Risks
- High glycemic index spikes blood sugar, potentially problematic for diabetics.
- May alter gut microbiota and mucus barrier in animal models, promoting inflammation.
- Often used as placebo but shows physiological effects in 64% of trials.
- Derived from wheat in some cases; gluten traces possible despite processing.

## Health Benefits
- Provides quick energy for athletes in sports drinks.
- Improves texture and shelf-life in foods without adding strong flavor.
- Easily digestible, suitable for those needing calorie-dense nutrition.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Tapioca starch or arrowroot for thickening.
- Resistant starch or inulin for fiber-like effects.
- Whole grain flours for natural bulk.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Wheat-derived versions may contain gluten traces; corn-derived typically gluten-free.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: 380 kcal
- Carbs: 95g (nearly all rapidly absorbed)
- Glycemic index: 85-135 (higher than sugar)
