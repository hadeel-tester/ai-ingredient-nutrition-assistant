# Stevia (E960)

## Summary
Stevia is a natural zero-calorie sweetener extracted from the leaves of the Stevia rebaudiana plant, 200-300 times sweeter than sugar. EFSA established an ADI of 4 mg/kg body weight for steviol glycosides after comprehensive safety evaluation. Widely used in beverages, desserts, and tabletop sweeteners as a sugar replacement.

## Health Risks
- Bitter aftertaste reported by some consumers.
- Possible mild digestive discomfort (bloating, nausea) at high doses.
- Animal studies showed reproductive effects at very high doses above human exposure.
- Limited long-term data on gut microbiome alterations.

## Health Benefits
- Zero calories and glycemic index suitable for diabetics and weight management.
- Antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties from steviol glycosides.
- May lower blood pressure and improve insulin sensitivity.
- Does not promote tooth decay unlike sugar.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Whole stevia leaf (less processed).
- Monk fruit extract (similar profile).
- Reducing sweetness overall with fruit/spices.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Rare hypersensitivity cases reported.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: 0 kcal
- Sweetness: 200-300x sugar
- Glycemic index: 0
