# Sodium Nitrate (E251)

## Summary
Sodium nitrate is an inorganic salt used in cured meats and some cheeses to inhibit bacterial growth, stabilize colour, and extend shelf life. It can also occur naturally in vegetables and drinking water as part of the nitrogen cycle. In foods it is gradually converted to sodium nitrite, which provides most of the preservative and colour-fixing effects.

## Health Risks
- At high intake, nitrates can convert to nitrites and then nitrosamines, some of which are carcinogenic, especially when processed meats are cooked at high temperatures.
- Excessive exposure can impair oxygen transport in the blood (methemoglobinemia), leading to headache, dizziness, shortness of breath, and in severe cases, collapse.
- High consumption of processed meats containing nitrate and nitrite has been associated with increased risk of colorectal cancer and cardiovascular disease in observational studies.
- Acute poisoning from large doses causes gastrointestinal symptoms, low blood pressure, abnormal heart rate, and can be fatal without treatment.

## Health Benefits
- In cured meats, sodium nitrate helps prevent growth of Clostridium botulinum, reducing the risk of botulism.
- Nitrate from vegetables can be converted to nitric oxide in the body, which supports blood vessel dilation and may help blood pressure regulation.
- Enables longer shelf life and food safety in certain meat products where refrigeration or hygiene is suboptimal.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Choosing fresh, unprocessed meats and fish instead of cured and processed options.
- Nitrate-free curing methods (for example, high-pressure processing, refrigeration, alternative preservatives) where available.
- Using herbs, spices, and smoking for flavour rather than relying on cured meats.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Does not contain protein; classic IgE-mediated allergy is unlikely, but intolerance or sensitivity to nitrates/nitrites can occur.
