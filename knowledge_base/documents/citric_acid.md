# Citric Acid (E330)

## Summary
Citric acid is a weak organic acid naturally found in citrus fruits, widely used as an acidulant, preservative, antioxidant synergist, and pH regulator in beverages, jams, candies, canned goods, and dairy products. Industrially produced by fermentation of Aspergillus niger mold using molasses or starch hydrolysates. GRAS by FDA and approved by EFSA/JECFA with no numerical ADI limit due to low toxicity and rapid metabolism.

## Health Risks
- Can erode tooth enamel with frequent exposure to acidic foods/drinks.
- Skin or eye irritation in pure concentrated form; food concentrations safe.
- Rare intolerance reactions causing stomach upset or skin rash.
- Synthetic production may leave trace mycotoxins, though tightly regulated.

## Health Benefits
- Enhances mineral absorption (iron, calcium, magnesium) from plant foods.
- Prevents enzymatic browning in cut fruits/vegetables.
- Synergistic antioxidant extending shelf life of vitamin C-rich products.
- pH adjustment improves microbial stability without excessive preservatives.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Natural fruit juices (lemon, lime) for acidity.
- Tartaric acid (E334) or malic acid (E296) from fruits.
- Vinegar or lactic acid fermentation for preservation.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Mold-derived but highly purified; no protein allergens remain.
- Suitable for all major dietary restrictions.
