# MSG (E621)

## Summary
MSG (monosodium glutamate) is the sodium salt of glutamic acid, a naturally occurring amino acid used as a flavor enhancer that provides umami taste in savory foods. Regulatory agencies worldwide including EFSA and WHO have established it as safe for consumption within specified limits after extensive review. Common in processed foods, soups, snacks, and Asian cuisine.

## Health Risks
- Chinese Restaurant Syndrome symptoms (headaches, flushing) reported anecdotally but not consistently reproducible in double-blind studies.
- High doses may cause transient blood pressure increase or numbness in sensitive individuals.
- Excessive intake contributes to overall sodium consumption.
- Animal studies using unrealistic high doses show brain effects irrelevant to human food use.

## Health Benefits
- Enhances flavor allowing salt reduction while maintaining taste.
- May increase meal enjoyment leading to greater vegetable consumption.
- Glutamate is naturally present in tomatoes, cheese, mushrooms, and soy sauce.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Natural glutamates from tomatoes, mushrooms, parmesan, or seaweed.
- Yeast extracts or hydrolyzed vegetable protein.
- Herbs, spices, and umami-rich ingredients like garlic.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Must be labeled when added as E621; naturally occurring glutamate exempt.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: 277 kcal
- Sodium: 12g (high)
- Glutamate: 72g
