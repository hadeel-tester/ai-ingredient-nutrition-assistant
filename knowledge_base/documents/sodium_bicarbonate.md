# Sodium Bicarbonate (E500)

## Summary
Sodium bicarbonate is a white crystalline powder used as a leavening agent in baking, reacting with acidic ingredients or heat to produce carbon dioxide gas that causes dough to rise. Functions as acidity regulator, antacid, and mild cleaning agent. Naturally occurs as nahcolite mineral; industrially produced via Solvay process. Safe for food use with no ADI limit established by EFSA/JECFA due to long history of safe consumption.

## Health Risks
- High sodium content contributes to total dietary sodium intake.
- Excessive medicinal use causes metabolic alkalosis, muscle spasms, and irritability.
- Large acute doses may lead to gas, bloating, or stomach rupture from CO2 production.
- Regular high intake linked to slower potassium excretion affecting blood pressure.

## Health Benefits
- Neutralizes stomach acid providing rapid heartburn relief.
- Supports athletic performance by buffering lactic acid during high-intensity exercise.
- May improve exercise capacity and delay fatigue in trained athletes.
- Dental applications reduce plaque and gingivitis as mild abrasive.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Baking powder (self-contained leavening with acid already included).
- Ammonium bicarbonate (E503) for moisture-sensitive baked goods.
- Yeast fermentation for natural bread rising.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Inorganic mineral salt; cannot trigger protein-based allergies.
- Safe for all major dietary restrictions.
