# Corn Starch

## Summary
Corn starch is a fine white powder extracted from the endosperm of corn kernels, used primarily as a thickening agent in sauces, gravies, soups, and baked goods. It creates glossy textures without cloudiness and serves as a gluten-free flour substitute in cooking. While versatile and inexpensive, it offers minimal nutrition beyond rapidly digestible carbohydrates.

## Health Risks
- High glycemic index (around 95) causes rapid blood sugar spikes, problematic for diabetes management or low-carb diets. 
- Refined product lacking fiber, protein, vitamins, and minerals, contributing empty calories. [medicinenet](https://www.medicinenet.com/cornstarch_is_it_healthier_than_flour_safe/article.htm)
- Large amounts may cause digestive discomfort like bloating or gas in sensitive individuals. [listonic](https://listonic.com/p/nutrition/corn-starch)
- Common in ultra-processed foods, indirectly linked to poor dietary patterns. [healthline](https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/is-cornstarch-bad-for-you)

## Health Benefits
- Gluten-free thickening option suitable for celiac disease or gluten intolerance. [chefadora](https://www.chefadora.com/ingredients/corn-starch)
- Provides quick-digesting carbohydrates for rapid energy needs. [chefadora](https://www.chefadora.com/ingredients/corn-starch)
- Neutral flavour preserves original taste of dishes. [listonic](https://listonic.com/p/nutrition/corn-starch)
- Cost-effective stabiliser extending shelf life in commercial products. [en.wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corn_starch)

## Healthier Alternatives
- Arrowroot powder for similar thickening with potentially lower glycemic impact.
- Tapioca starch or potato starch as gluten-free substitutes.
- Whole grain flours (e.g., rice flour, oat flour) or xanthan gum for nutrient-dense thickening; prioritising vegetable purees or reductions eliminates need for refined starches.

## Allergen Info
- Naturally gluten-free, but check processing for cross-contamination risks in celiac patients. [wisdomlib](https://www.wisdomlib.org/ingredients/corn-starch)
- Not a major allergen.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: ~381 kcal
- Carbohydrates: ~91g (minimal fiber, sugars, protein, or fat). [medicinenet](https://www.medicinenet.com/cornstarch_is_it_healthier_than_flour_safe/article.htm)
