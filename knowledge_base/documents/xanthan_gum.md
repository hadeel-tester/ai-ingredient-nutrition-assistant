# Xanthan Gum (E415)

## Summary
Xanthan gum is a high molecular weight polysaccharide produced by fermenting carbohydrates (often corn, wheat, or sugar) with the bacterium Xanthomonas campestris, then purified and dried into a fine powder. It is widely used as a thickener, stabilizer, and emulsifier in gluten-free baking, sauces, dressings, dairy products, and beverages. Regulatory bodies including EFSA and JECFA have evaluated xanthan gum as safe at typical food use levels and did not establish a numerical ADI due to its low toxicity and minimal absorption.

## Health Risks
- Large doses can cause increased stool frequency, gas, and softer stools due to its soluble fiber-like properties.
- Extremely high intake may alter gut microbiota composition, though such doses are far above normal dietary exposure.
- Some products may contain residual proteins from the fermentation substrate (corn, soy, wheat), theoretically posing issues for highly sensitive individuals.
- For infants under 16 weeks, EFSA requested additional data and specific assessment due to limited evidence in this age group.

## Health Benefits
- Acts as a soluble fiber contributing to stool bulk and may support regular bowel movements.
- Helps create structure and elasticity in gluten-free baking, improving texture and volume.
- Stabilizes emulsions and suspensions, reducing the need for higher fat content in some foods.
- Can improve mouthfeel in low-calorie and reduced-fat products.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Guar gum or locust bean gum as natural thickeners.
- Psyllium husk for gluten-free baking and additional fiber.
- Chia or ground flax seeds for thickening and binding in some recipes.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Usually considered safe for people with celiac disease when produced from gluten-free substrates, but extremely sensitive individuals may prefer certified gluten-free products.
- Allergen risk primarily relates to possible traces from the carbohydrate source used in fermentation.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: ~300 kcal
- Carbohydrates: ~80g (mostly non-digestible polysaccharides)
- Fiber: high, largely non-absorbed
- Protein and fat: negligible
