# Soy Protein Isolate

## Summary
Soy protein isolate is a highly refined protein powder containing at least 90% protein, extracted from defatted soybeans through alkaline extraction and precipitation. It provides all essential amino acids making it a complete plant-based protein suitable for muscle building, vegetarian diets, and food fortification. FDA recognizes 25g daily intake may reduce heart disease risk when replacing animal proteins in low-fat diets.

## Health Risks
- Soy allergy affects 0.4% of children; isolate contains minimal soy protein but reactions possible in highly sensitive individuals.
- Phytoestrogens (isoflavones) present in small amounts; no hormonal effects shown in moderate human consumption.
- Highly processed using hexane extraction; trace solvent residues theoretically possible but tightly regulated.
- Digestive discomfort (bloating, gas) common when consumed in large amounts without gradual introduction.

## Health Benefits
- Complete protein with high PDCAAS score comparable to whey for muscle protein synthesis.
- Clinical trials show cholesterol reduction (5-6% LDL) and improved blood vessel function.
- Satiety effects support weight management when replacing higher-calorie proteins.
- Iron absorption enhanced compared to many plant proteins.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Whole soybeans, edamame, or tempeh for fiber and phytochemicals.
- Pea protein isolate or rice protein blends for variety.
- Hemp seeds providing balanced omega-3/6 profile.

## Allergen Info
- Soy is top-14 EU allergen requiring clear labeling.
- Highly refined isolate typically tolerated by most soy-allergic individuals.
- Always check for cross-contamination in manufacturing facilities.
