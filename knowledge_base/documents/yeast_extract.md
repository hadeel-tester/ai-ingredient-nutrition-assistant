# Yeast Extract

## Summary
Yeast extract is a food ingredient derived from yeast cells (usually Saccharomyces cerevisiae) through autolysis or enzymatic breakdown, producing a savory umami flavor concentrate rich in amino acids, nucleotides, peptides, and B vitamins. Commonly used as a flavor enhancer in snacks, soups, gravies, and vegetarian products as a natural alternative to MSG. Provides natural glutamate levels similar to tomatoes, cheese, and mushrooms.

## Health Risks
- High sodium content contributes to overall salt intake.
- Contains free glutamate; rare sensitivity reactions similar to MSG in susceptible individuals.
- High purine content may elevate uric acid levels in gout sufferers.
- Digestive discomfort possible when consumed in large amounts due to nucleotide content.

## Health Benefits
- Rich source of B vitamins supporting energy metabolism and nervous system function.
- Provides complete amino acid profile with high biological value protein content.
- Nucleotides support immune function and gut health.
- Low-calorie flavor enhancer allowing salt reduction in recipes.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Mushrooms, tomatoes, or parmesan for natural umami.
- Seaweed (kombu) or fermented soy products.
- Herbs and spices for flavor without processed additives.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Safe for gluten-free, nut-free, and dairy-free diets.
- Rare yeast sensitivity exists but distinct from common food allergies.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: 250-350 kcal
- Protein: 30-50g
- Sodium: high (3-5g)
- B vitamins: very high (thiamine, riboflavin, niacin)
