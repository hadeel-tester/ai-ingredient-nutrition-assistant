# Titanium Dioxide (E171)

## Summary
Titanium dioxide (E171) is a bright white powdered mineral used as a color additive to enhance whiteness and opacity in candies, chewing gum, baked goods, sauces, and dairy products. EFSA's 2021 re-evaluation concluded genotoxicity concerns could not be ruled out, leading to EU-wide ban as food additive from 2022. Nanoparticles (10-30nm) can penetrate gut barrier and accumulate in organs.

## Health Risks
- Genotoxicity concerns; EFSA cannot rule out DNA damage potential.
- Accumulates in liver, spleen, and intestines with slow clearance.
- Promotes gut inflammation, barrier dysfunction, and microbiota dysbiosis.
- Animal studies show increased tumor promotion, adenomas, and preneoplastic lesions.

## Health Benefits
- Provides bright white color improving visual appeal of foods.
- Opaque coating prevents ingredient separation and discoloration.
- High refractive index gives superior whiteness compared to alternatives.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Calcium carbonate (E170) for whitening.
- Rice starch, rice bran wax, or carnauba wax for coatings.
- Natural color blends from vegetables and spices.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Mineral compound; no protein-based allergic reactions possible.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: 0 kcal
- Provides no nutritional value
- Particle size: typically 200-300nm, nano-fraction 10-30nm
