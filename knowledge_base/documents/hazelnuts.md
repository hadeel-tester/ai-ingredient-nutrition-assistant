# Hazelnuts

## Summary
Hazelnuts are nutrient-dense tree nuts rich in monounsaturated fats, vitamin E, manganese, and antioxidants, providing heart health benefits and anti-inflammatory effects. A 28g serving delivers 176 calories with 17g healthy fats, 4g protein, and significant fiber. Regular consumption links to improved cholesterol profiles, reduced oxidative stress, and better metabolic health.

## Health Risks
- Major allergen causing severe reactions including anaphylaxis in sensitive individuals.
- High calorie density contributes to weight gain if overconsumed.
- Oxalates may contribute to kidney stone formation in susceptible people.
- Aflatoxin contamination risk from improper storage.

## Health Benefits
- Monounsaturated fats (oleic acid) improve LDL/HDL cholesterol ratio.
- High antioxidant content reduces inflammation and oxidative damage.
- Supports cardiovascular health through blood pressure regulation.
- Prebiotic fiber promotes beneficial gut bacteria growth.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Almonds (similar profile, lower oxalates).
- Walnuts (additional omega-3s).
- Seeds (pumpkin, sunflower) for nut-free options.

## Allergen Info
- Top-14 EU allergen requiring clear labeling.
- Roasted nuts have lower allergenicity than raw.
- Cross-reactivity common with birch pollen allergy.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: 628 kcal
- Fat: 61g (mostly monounsaturated)
- Protein: 15g
- Fiber: 10g
- Vitamin E: 21mg (104% DV)
