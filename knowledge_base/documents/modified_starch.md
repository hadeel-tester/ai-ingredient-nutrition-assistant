# Modified Starch (E1404-E1452)

## Summary
Modified starch refers to starches chemically, physically, or enzymatically altered to improve functionality as thickeners, stabilizers, and texture modifiers in processed foods. EFSA re-evaluated 12 types in 2017 and confirmed safety with ADI "not specified" due to low toxicity and near-complete digestion. Common in sauces, snacks, and ready meals.

## Health Risks
- High glycemic index causes rapid blood sugar spikes.
- Some animal studies show gut permeability changes at very high doses.
- Wheat-derived types may retain trace gluten despite processing.
- Over-reliance in processed foods contributes to poor dietary quality.

## Health Benefits
- Enhances food texture and stability without strong flavor impact.
- Freeze-thaw stable for frozen ready meals.
- Easily digested providing quick carbohydrate energy.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Native (unmodified) starches like corn or potato.
- Guar gum or xanthan for thickening.
- Whole food thickeners like pureed vegetables.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Wheat/corn-derived versions require allergen labeling if traces present.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: 350-380 kcal
- Carbohydrates: 85-95g
- Glycemic index: 80-110
