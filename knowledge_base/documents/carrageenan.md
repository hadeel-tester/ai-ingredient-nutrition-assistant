# Carrageenan (E407)

## Summary
Carrageenan is a natural polysaccharide extracted from red seaweed, used widely as a gelling agent, thickener, and stabiliser in processed foods. It appears in dairy products, plant-based milks, ice cream, deli meats, and sauces to improve texture, prevent separation, and enhance mouthfeel. While approved as safe by regulatory authorities, it has been controversial due to animal studies suggesting potential gut inflammation at high doses.

## Health Risks
- Animal studies indicate degraded carrageenan (poligeenan) can cause intestinal inflammation and ulcers, though food-grade carrageenan is chemically distinct and present at much lower levels.
- Some research links high intake of food emulsifiers including carrageenan to altered gut microbiota, increased gut permeability, and metabolic issues like glucose intolerance.
- Sensitive individuals may experience bloating, diarrhea, or irritable bowel symptoms, particularly from products with multiple emulsifiers.
- Found predominantly in ultra-processed foods, contributing to overall dietary patterns associated with poorer health outcomes.

## Health Benefits
- Enables low-fat dairy and plant-based alternatives to achieve creamy textures without excess calories or saturated fat.
- Improves freeze-thaw stability in ice cream and frozen desserts, reducing waste from texture breakdown.
- Functions as a vegetarian gelling agent, replacing animal-derived gelatine in desserts and confectionery.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Agar-agar from red algae provides similar gelling properties with potentially less digestive impact.
- Guar gum, locust bean gum, or xanthan gum offer thickening without the controversy surrounding carrageenan.
- Prioritising homemade or minimally processed foods naturally eliminates the need for commercial stabilisers.

## Allergen Info
- Not among the EU top-14 allergens.
- Derived from seaweed, it contains no significant protein content that would trigger typical food allergies.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: ~250 kcal (mostly indigestible fibre)
- Provides no vitamins, minerals, protein, or digestible carbohydrates; purely functional additive
