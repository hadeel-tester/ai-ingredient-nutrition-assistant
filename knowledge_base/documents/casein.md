# Casein

## Summary
Casein is the primary protein in cow's milk (about 80% of total milk protein), used as a food ingredient in processed cheeses, protein supplements, coffee whiteners, baked goods, and meat products. It functions as an emulsifier, stabiliser, and nutrient source due to its slow-digesting properties and ability to form gels. While nutritionally valuable, it poses significant allergy risks and is unsuitable for vegans or those avoiding dairy.

## Health Risks
- Major milk allergen containing multiple allergenic epitopes that can trigger IgE-mediated reactions ranging from mild (hives, itching) to severe anaphylaxis.
- Some individuals experience digestive discomfort (bloating, gas) due to poor casein digestibility compared with whey protein.
- A1 beta-casein (common in most cow breeds) has been controversially linked to digestive issues and inflammation in sensitive populations, though evidence is mixed.
- High-heat processed casein products may form advanced glycation end-products that contribute to inflammation when consumed regularly.

## Health Benefits
- Slow-digesting "time-release" protein provides sustained amino acid delivery (rich in leucine), supporting overnight muscle repair and satiety.
- Complete protein containing all essential amino acids, making it valuable for muscle building and recovery when combined with resistance training.
- Naturally high in calcium and phosphorus when derived from milk, supporting bone health.
- Forms protective curds in the stomach, potentially aiding nutrient absorption in some populations.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Plant-based proteins like pea protein isolate, soy protein, or rice protein blends for vegans and those avoiding dairy allergens.
- Whey protein concentrate (from milk) digests faster for post-workout recovery while avoiding casein's slower absorption and allergy profile.
- Whole food sources like lentils, chickpeas, quinoa, or eggs provide complete proteins without additives or processing concerns.

## Allergen Info
- Casein is one of the major milk allergens (one of EU top-14 priority allergens).
- Even hydrolysed or modified casein can retain allergenic potential; strict avoidance required for milk-allergic individuals.
- Cross-contamination risk high in facilities processing both casein and other milk-derived ingredients.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: ~380 kcal
- Protein: ~85-90g (complete amino acid profile, high in glutamine and BCAAs)
- Calcium: ~700-900mg (when derived from milk sources)
