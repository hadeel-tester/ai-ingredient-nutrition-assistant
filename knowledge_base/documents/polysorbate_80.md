# Polysorbate 80 (E433)

## Summary
Polysorbate 80 is a synthetic non-ionic surfactant and emulsifier derived from sorbitol and oleic acid, used to blend oil and water in ice cream, sauces, dressings, and processed foods. Approved by EFSA, FDA, and JECFA as safe with maximum permitted levels typically 500-1000 mg/kg in foods. Improves texture stability and prevents ingredient separation.

## Health Risks
- High doses in animal studies cause intestinal inflammation and altered gut permeability.
- Potential to enhance absorption of fat-soluble toxins or allergens.
- Rare reports of hypersensitivity reactions in sensitive individuals.
- Ethylene oxide residues theoretically possible from manufacturing but regulated.

## Health Benefits
- Enables stable low-fat formulations in processed foods.
- Improves mouthfeel and creaminess in ice cream and spreads.
- Functions as solubilizer for vitamins and flavors in supplements.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Lecithin (soy or sunflower) as natural emulsifier.
- Gum arabic for beverage emulsions.
- Simple ingredient reformulation avoiding emulsifiers.

## Allergen Info
- Not a top-14 EU allergen.
- Vegetable-derived; suitable for most dietary restrictions.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: ~650 kcal
- Fat: 81g (mostly oleic acid)
- No significant vitamins/minerals
