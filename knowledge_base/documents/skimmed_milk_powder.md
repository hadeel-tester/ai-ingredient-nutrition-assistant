# Skimmed Milk Powder

## Summary
Skimmed milk powder is dehydrated skimmed cow's milk with fat content reduced to less than 1.5%, containing 34-36% high-quality protein, 50-52% lactose, and essential minerals like calcium and phosphorus. Used widely in food manufacturing for standardization, texture enhancement, and protein fortification in bakery, confectionery, yogurt, cheese, and recombined milk products. Long shelf life and easy reconstitution make it valuable for industrial and developing market applications.

## Health Risks
- Contains lactose causing digestive issues for lactose-intolerant individuals.
- Milk protein allergy risk including casein and whey sensitivities.
- High sodium versions contribute to dietary sodium intake.
- Oxysterols formed during spray drying may promote oxidation.

## Health Benefits
- High biological value protein supports muscle maintenance and growth.
- Excellent calcium source (1200mg/100g) for bone health.
- Provides essential vitamins B2, B12 supporting energy metabolism.
- Prebiotic lactose selectively feeds beneficial gut bacteria.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Whole milk powder for natural fat-soluble vitamins.
- Plant-based protein powders (pea, rice) for vegans.
- Liquid skim milk when fresh availability allows.

## Allergen Info
- Top-14 EU allergen (milk) requiring clear labeling.
- Contains both casein and whey proteins triggering different allergies.
- Cross-contamination risk in facilities processing other allergens.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: 360 kcal
- Protein: 36g
- Fat: 1g
- Carbohydrates: 52g (mostly lactose)
