# Soy Lecithin (E322)

## Summary
Soy lecithin is a mixture of phospholipids extracted from soybeans, widely used as an emulsifier, stabilizer, and anti-caking agent in chocolate, baked goods, margarines, and many processed foods. It helps blend fats and water, improving texture and shelf life without adding noticeable flavour. In small amounts it is generally considered safe for most people, though it may contain trace soy proteins relevant for allergies.

## Health Risks
- May contain small amounts of soy protein, which can trigger reactions in highly sensitive soy-allergic individuals.
- Often derived from genetically modified soy unless specifically labeled non-GMO or organic.
- Large supplemental doses can cause mild gastrointestinal upset such as diarrhea, nausea, or abdominal discomfort in some people.
- Very high choline intake from concentrated supplements may increase TMAO levels, a potential cardiovascular risk marker, in susceptible individuals.

## Health Benefits
- Source of choline and phospholipids that support cell membrane structure and liver function.
- May help lower LDL (“bad”) cholesterol and support HDL (“good”) cholesterol when used in supplemental doses.
- Emulsifying properties can support intestinal mucus and barrier function, potentially benefiting some digestive conditions.
- Used in some studies to support cognitive function, stress response, and menopausal symptom relief.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Sunflower lecithin for those avoiding soy.
- Minimal-ingredient foods that rely on natural fat content rather than added emulsifiers.
- Whole-food sources of healthy fats and phospholipids such as eggs (for non-vegan diets), nuts, seeds, and avocados.

## Allergen Info
- Derived from soy, which is a top allergen; however, refined soy lecithin typically contains very low soy protein.
- Most soy-allergic individuals tolerate soy lecithin, but highly sensitive people should consult an allergist and check labels carefully.
- Must be declared as a soy-derived ingredient under EU and many other labelling rules.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: ~700 kcal
- Fat: ~70g (mainly phospholipids and polyunsaturated fats)
- Choline: high (exact content varies by product)
- Carbohydrate, protein, fiber: low, product-dependent
