# Whey Protein

## Summary
Whey protein is a complete protein derived from milk during cheese production, containing all essential amino acids with high bioavailability. Available as concentrate (70-80% protein), isolate (90%+ protein), or hydrolysate (pre-digested), it is popular for muscle building, weight management, and sports nutrition. Rapid absorption makes it ideal post-workout for muscle protein synthesis.

## Health Risks
- Contains lactose causing digestive issues in lactose-intolerant individuals.
- Milk allergy risk; not suitable for those with dairy allergies.
- Excessive intake (>2g/kg body weight daily) may stress kidneys in those with pre-existing kidney disease.
- Some products contain added sugars, artificial flavors, or heavy metals from poor manufacturing.

## Health Benefits
- Superior for muscle protein synthesis due to high leucine content.
- Supports weight loss by increasing satiety and preserving lean mass.
- May improve cholesterol profile and reduce blood pressure.
- Boosts glutathione antioxidant levels supporting immune function.

## Healthier Alternatives
- Casein protein for slower digestion.
- Plant blends (pea, rice, hemp) for vegans.
- Whole food sources like Greek yogurt, eggs, cottage cheese.

## Allergen Info
- Derived from milk; top-14 EU allergen.
- Whey isolate contains minimal lactose but still triggers milk protein allergies.
- Clear labeling required; cross-contamination risk in facilities.

## Nutritional Snapshot (per 100g)
- Calories: 400 kcal
- Protein: 80-90g
- Fat: 2-7g
- Carbohydrates: 3-10g (varies by type)
